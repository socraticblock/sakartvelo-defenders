Historical Chronicle

The Georgian Chronicle

Every era, every hero, every battle -- documented in the historical record. This is the reference companion to Sakartvelo Defenders.

The Ten Eras

3,000 Years of Georgian History

Sakartvelo Defenders spans from the legendary kingdom of Colchis to modern democratic Georgia. Each era introduces new gameplay mechanics, towers, enemies, and heroes.

Era 0: Ancient Colchis

~600 BC - ~100 BC

The legendary kingdom of the Golden Fleece. The Medea statue in Batumi overlooks the Black Sea coast. Explore the archaeological site of Vani, gold-panning with sheep fleeces, and battle devi -- many-headed ogres from Georgian myth. Dense Colchian forests, river deltas, and Black Sea coastal fortifications.

Key Battles & Events

  • Argonaut Expedition (mythological) -- Jason seeks the Golden Fleece
  • Medea's Flight -- the sorceress flees with Jason
  • Colchis vs. Persian invasions -- defense of the Golden Fleece treasury

Gameplay Mechanics

Tutorial era. Simple tower placement, basic resource management, introduction to hero abilities. No synergy mechanics yet.

Era 1: Kingdom of Iberia

~300 BC - 630 AD

The ancient Georgian kingdom east of the Surami Mountains. Pharnavaz I establishes the Iberian kingdom after Alexander the Great's campaigns. St. Nino brings Christianity to Georgia in 327 AD, transforming the kingdom's identity and alliances.

Key Battles & Events

  • Battle of Pantopolis -- Iberian forces vs. Roman intervention
  • Christianization of Georgia -- St. Nino's mission
  • Vakhtang Gorgasali's campaigns -- founding of Tbilisi
  • Defense against Sassanian Persia

Heroes

King Pharnavaz (Era 1) -- Kingdom-wide economy and cost reduction buffs. Saint Nino (Era 1) -- Tower shields and enemy slow aura. Vakhtang Gorgasali (Era 1) -- Area damage boost for towers.

Gameplay Mechanics

Hero ability system introduced. First synergy combos (Archer + Cavalry). Cavalry Outpost tower introduced.

Era 2: Age of Invasions

~630 - 1089 AD

The medieval crucible. First Arab capture of Tbilisi (~645 AD), formal Emirate established 736 CE. Didi Turkoba (Great Turkish Onslaught) devastates the countryside. Bagrat III the Unifier crowns a unified Georgian kingdom in 1008. Mountain fortresses hold the line against Arab and Seljuk forces while the lowlands burn.

Key Battles & Events

  • Siege of Tbilisi (627 AD) -- failed Arab siege of the Iberian capital
  • Battle of Didgori (1121 AD) -- David IV defeats the Seljuk Turks
  • Arab Emirate of Tbilisi -- occupation and resistance

Gameplay Mechanics

Siege Tower introduced (heavy damage vs. armored targets). Siege units introduced (battering rams attack towers directly). First armor-resistance mechanics. Synergy: Siege + Cavalry = +20% Armor Shred.

Era 3: The Georgian Golden Age

~1089 - 1225 AD

The zenith of Georgian power and culture. David IV's military reforms and Queen Tamar's diplomatic genius create an empire stretching from the Black Sea to the Caspian. Zakare Zakarian leads the armies that liberate Ani. Tamar's two marriages -- Yuri Bogolyubsky then David Soslan -- shape the dynasty. The Empire of Trebizond becomes a Georgian client state. Cathedrals, poetry, and battlefield engineering flourish.

Key Battles & Events

  • Battle of Didgori (1121 AD) -- David IV's decisive victory over the Seljuks
  • Battle of Basiani (1204 AD) -- Queen Tamar's forces defeat the Seljuks
  • Foundation of Gelati Academy -- UNESCO World Heritage
  • Liberation of Ani -- Zakare Zakarian's forces capture the great city
  • Empire of Trebizond established as Georgian client state (1204 AD)

Heroes

David IV, David the Builder (Era 3) -- Global damage buff and upgrade cost reduction. Queen Tamar (Era 3) -- Resource generation and tower invulnerability. Shota Rustaveli (Era 3) -- Global attack speed increase.

Gameplay Mechanics

Church Shrine Tower introduced (crowd control, buffs, debuffs). Full synergy system active. Elevation mechanic introduced. Multiple synergy combos: Church Shrine+Wall, Church Shrine+Archer, Catapult+Archer.

Era 4: The Mongol Catastrophe

~1225 - 1500 AD

The empire collapses under the Mongol onslaught. Jalal ad-Din Mingburnu devastates Georgia before the Mongols themselves arrive. The kingdom fragments into smaller principalities, holding on in mountain fortresses while the lowlands are occupied.

Key Battles & Events

  • Mongol invasion of Georgia (1236-1240 AD) -- total devastation
  • Battle of Yassich (1242 AD) -- Georgia submits to Mongol rule
  • George V the Brilliant -- recovers independence from Mongol rule, reunites the kingdom
  • Timur's invasions (1386-1403 AD) -- second catastrophic devastation of Georgia

Gameplay Mechanics

Mastery scroll system introduced (permanent NFT upgrades). Surviving the wave is harder -- enemies are faster and hit harder. Mountain fortress maps with elevation advantages.

Era 5: Between Empires

~1500 - 1801 AD

Caught between the Ottoman and Persian empires, Georgian kings play diplomacy against occupation. King Erekle II briefly achieves independence for Kartli-Kakheti before Russia absorbs Georgia entirely.

Key Battles & Events

  • Battle of Telavi (1769 AD) -- Erekle II defeats Persian forces
  • Battle of Aspindza (1510 AD) -- Georgian victory over Ottomans
  • Permanent Russian protectorate (1783 AD) -- Treaty of Georgievsk

Heroes

King Erekle II (Era 5) -- Enemy damage reduction and emergency resource generation.

Gameplay Mechanics

Gunpowder Tower introduced (long-range piercing projectiles). Tower upgrades begin costing SAKART (in addition to gold). Diplomatic/damage-reduction enemy mechanics.

Era 6: Russian Empire

~1801 - 1918 AD

Annexed by Russia, Georgia experiences cultural suppression and modernization simultaneously. Georgian cultural figures -- Ilia Chavchavadze, Akaki Tsereteli -- lead a national awakening while Russian military technology transforms the landscape.

Key Battles & Events

  • Annexation by Russian Empire (1801 AD)
  • 1905 Russian Revolution -- Georgian participation
  • Cultural renaissance and national awakening

Heroes

Ilia Chavchavadze (Era 6) -- Tower efficiency and resource generation.

Gameplay Mechanics

Industrial Tower introduced (rapid-fire multi-target suppression). Bunker-like defensive structures. Modernized infantry enemies.

Era 7: First Democratic Republic

~1918 - 1921 AD

Georgia declares independence in 1918 following the Russian Revolution. Noe Jordania's Social-Democratic government lasts just three years before Soviet invasion, but it establishes the first democratic constitution in the region.

Key Battles & Events

  • Independence Declaration (May 26, 1918 AD)
  • Transcaucasian Federation (1918) -- brief union with Armenia and Azerbaijan
  • British military presence (1918-1920) -- protecting Batumi and the oil route
  • League of Nations application -- postponed, never approved
  • Soviet Invasion (February 1921 AD) -- fall of the Republic

Heroes

Noe Jordania (Era 7) -- Global tower strength boost and emergency resource generation.

Gameplay Mechanics

Shortest era (only 10 levels). Rapid escalation of Soviet vs. Georgian forces. Introduction of early automatic weapons.

Era 8: The Soviet Century

~1921 - ~1991 AD

Soviet occupation and cultural suppression. WWII brings Georgian soldiers to the Eastern Front. The independence movement grows through the 1970s and 1980s, culminating in the Singing Revolution and eventual independence.

Key Battles & Events

  • World War II -- Georgian soldiers on the Eastern Front
  • 1957 independence protests -- suppressed by Soviet authorities
  • Singing Revolution (late 1980s) -- peaceful independence movement
  • Independence (April 9, 1991 AD)

Gameplay Mechanics

Bunker Tower introduced (extreme armor and survivability). Survival mode mechanics introduced. Soviet military tech vs. Georgian resistance.

Era 9: Modern Georgia

~1991 - Present

Independent democratic Georgia rebuilds while facing separatist conflicts and Russian pressure. The 2008 war demonstrates modern drone warfare. Georgia today is a European-style democracy with NATO and EU aspirations.

Key Battles & Events

  • War in Abkhazia (1992-1993 AD) -- separatist conflict
  • Rose Revolution (2003 AD) -- bloodless democratic transition
  • 2008 Russo-Georgian War -- modern drone warfare, precision weapons

Gameplay Mechanics

Tech Tower introduced (precision strikes, drone support, target marking). Modern drone enemies. Most complex era -- combines all previous mechanics. Endgame content.

Boss Encounters

Multi-Phase Boss Battles

Every 5th level features a named historical or mythological boss with multi-phase mechanics. Each phase requires a different strategic approach.

Era 0 -- Aeetes, King of Colchis

Phase 1: Dragon form. Phase 2: Golden Fleece barrier (projectile reflection). Phase 3: Rages when below 25% HP -- increased speed and damage.

Era 1 -- The Arab Emir of Tbilisi

Phase 1: Leads cavalry charges. Phase 2: Fortifies position -- calls in infantry reinforcements. Phase 3: Accepts defeat -- retreats to fight another day.

Era 2 -- Seljuk Turk Warlord

Phase 1: Siege weapons. Phase 2: Elite Janissary guard. Phase 3: Desperate last stand.

Era 3 -- Jalal ad-Din Mingburnu

Phase 1: Mongol cavalry swarm. Phase 2: Archer volley formation. Phase 3: Duel -- requires hero ability timing.

Era 4 -- General Chormaqan and Timur (Tamerlane)

Phase 1: Heavy cavalry. Phase 2: Siege engines. Phase 3: Must be killed quickly -- respawns with double HP after 30 seconds. General Chormaqan led the actual 1236-1243 conquest; Timur (Tamerlane) appears as second boss representing later Mongol destruction.

Era 5 -- Agha Mohammad Khan

Phase 1: Persian heavy cavalry. Phase 2: Elite Qajar guard. Phase 3: Uses terrain -- summons landslide that destroys a section of your maze.

Era 6 -- Russian Imperial General

Phase 1: Infantry line. Phase 2: Artillery bombardment (kills towers in AoE). Phase 3: Cossack cavalry flanking maneuver.

Era 7 -- Soviet Red Army Commander

Phase 1: T-34 tank. Phase 2: Infantry with anti-tank weapons. Phase 3: Air support (first flying boss).

Era 8 -- Soviet General

Phase 1: Mechanized infantry. Phase 2: Mi-24 helicopter gunship. Phase 3: Full military assault -- tanks, air, infantry.

Era 9 -- Russian Modern General

Phase 1: Drone swarm. Phase 2: Precision artillery (telegraphed, destructible). Phase 3: Electronic warfare -- disables random tower for 10 seconds. Survive the full assault to win.
