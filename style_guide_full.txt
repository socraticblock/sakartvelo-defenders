11. Art Direction
1.1 Overall Style: 2D Isometric Cel-Shaded
Sakartvelo Defenders uses a 2D isometric art style with cel-shaded lighting. All game assets,  
including towers, enemies, heroes, maps, and UI elements, share this foundational visual  
language. The isometric perspective provides clear spatial awareness for tower placement  
decisions while maintaining a warm, illustrated quality that suits the historical subject  
matter. Cel-shaded lighting creates sharp shadow boundaries and flat color fills that give the  
art a distinctive, hand-illustrated appearance consistent with AI-generated art pipelines.
The art style draws inspiration from illustrated history books, museum dioramas, and  
traditional Georgian miniature painting. Every asset should feel like it belongs in a high-
quality history textbook rather than a generic mobile game. The color palette for each era is  
carefully curated to reflect the historical period's visual character, while maintaining overall  
cohesion through shared rendering techniques (consistent shadow direction, outline weight,  
and texture density).
All art is generated using AI image generation tools, guided by this style guide. Consistency  
is maintained through standardized prompt templates, era-specific keyword libraries, and a  
rigorous review process against the consistency checklist in Section 13. No hand-drawn art is  
used; instead, the style guide ensures that AI-generated assets from different generation  
sessions are visually indistinguishable when placed side by side.
1.2 Rendering Rules
Shadow Direction: All shadows cast to the lower-right at a 45-degree angle, consistent with  
the isometric camera position. Shadow color is a desaturated version of the object's base  
color at 40% opacity. Shadow offset is proportional to object height: taller objects cast longer  
shadows.
Outline Weight: All objects have a 2px dark outline (#1A1A1A) on all edges except where  
objects overlap the ground plane. Ground shadows do not have outlines. Character outlines  
are slightly thicker (3px) for readability at small sizes.
Color Fills: Flat color fills with no gradients except for metallic surfaces (swords, armor)  
which use a simple two-tone highlight/shadow gradient. Color values must match the era-
specific palette exactly; no deviations are permitted without explicit approval.
Texture: Minimal texture usage. Stone surfaces may include a subtle noise overlay at 5-10%  
opacity. Wood grain is represented by 2-3 parallel lines rather than photorealistic texture.

2. Global UI Palette
The global UI palette is used for all user interface elements that are not era-specific: menus,  
buttons, HUD elements, settings panels, the marketplace, and loading screens. Era-specific  
content (maps, towers, enemies, heroes) uses the era palettes defined in Section 3. The global  
UI palette is designed to be neutral and readable, complementing any era's color scheme  
without clashing.
Background:  #1A1A1A (Dark) / #F5F0EB (Light) — Dark mode is default; light mode  
available in settings.
Primary Text: #FFFFFF on dark / #1A1A1A on light.
Secondary Text: #B0B8C0 on dark / #6B7280 on light.
Accent: #E67E22 (Warm Orange) — Used for interactive elements, selected states, and call-
to-action buttons.

actions.

Disabled: #4A5568 on dark / #D1D5DB on light — Inactive buttons and locked content.

3.1 Era 0: Ancient Colchis
The Colchis palette is dominated by deep forest greens and rich earth tones, reflecting the  
lush  Black  Sea  coastal  environment  described  in  Greek  sources.  The  golden  accent  
(#D4A017) references the Golden Fleece legend and the historical gold-mining activities of  
the Colchian civilization. The base green (#2D5A3D) evokes the dense Colchian forests that  
were a major strategic asset in ancient warfare, providing timber for fortifications and  
concealment for defenders.
Tower skins in this era use the Stone/Earth palette for foundations and the Base green for  
wooden superstructures. The Accent gold is used for decorative elements on important  
structures and for Medea's hero design. Enemy designs for tribal raiders use the Shadow  
palette for leather armor and the Accent gold for jewelry and ceremonial items. Map  
backgrounds feature the Sky blue for the Black Sea horizon, Vegetation green for dense  
forests, and Water blue for rivers and coastline.

3.2 Era 1: Kingdom of Iberia
The Iberian palette shifts to warm browns and muted golds, reflecting the stone architecture  
and arid highland landscapes of early Georgian statehood. The period covers the transition  
from pagan to Christian Georgia, and the palette balances the warm earth tones of pre-
Christian architecture with the nascent use of cross symbols in decorative elements. The

5highlight brown (#8D6E63) represents the sandstone used in Iberian fortresses, while the  
accent gold (#C8A96E) evokes the royal Bagrationi regalia that would become iconic in later  
eras.
Tower skins in this era feature more substantial stone construction compared to Era 0. Archer  
towers are built on stone foundations with wooden platforms, using the Base and Stone/Earth  
colors. The introduction of Christianity is reflected in the subtle use of cross motifs on tower  
decorations, particularly on the Shrine tower type introduced later. Enemy designs for  
Roman and Persian forces use distinct palette subsets: Roman enemies use cooler grays  
within the Stone/Earth range, while Persian enemies introduce the warm red-brown that will  
become dominant in Era 2.
3.3 Era 2: Age of Invasions
The Age of Invasions palette is deliberately harsh and desaturated, reflecting the devastation  
of the Arab conquest period and the ongoing military pressure on Georgian lands. The base  
brown (#5C3A21) is darker and more austere than Era 1, representing fortresses under siege  
and a civilization under threat. The accent gold (#DAA520) is muted and battle-worn,  
symbolizing the resilience of Georgian culture under occupation rather than its prosperity.
This era's visual language is defined by contrast between the worn, battle-scarred Georgian  
architecture and the imposing, geometric forms of Arab military architecture. Enemy designs  
for Arab forces use the Accent and Shadow palette for their distinctive desert military colors,  
while Georgian defensive structures use the muted Base and Stone/Earth colors to convey a  
sense of endurance under pressure. Map backgrounds feature darker, more dramatic sky  
colors (#B8860B) suggesting dust storms and the smoke of burning cities.
3.4 Era 3: The Georgian Golden Age
The Golden Age palette is the richest and most vibrant in the game, reflecting the historical  
period  of  Georgia's  greatest  power  and  cultural  achievement.  The  deep  green  base  
(#1A4D2E)  represents  the  lush  Georgian  countryside  during  a  period  of  agricultural  
prosperity. The accent gold (#D4AF37) is pure and brilliant, symbolizing the legendary  
wealth of Queen Tamar's court. The accent red (#C41E3A) represents both the Georgian  
cross (adopted during this period) and the military banners of the unified Georgian army.
Tower skins in this era are the most ornate in the game. Fortresses feature intricate stone  
carvings, gold decorative elements, and the distinctive Georgian cross motif. The Shrine  
tower type, introduced in this era, uses the full palette to create visually stunning religious  
structures with gold domes, red crosses, and green surrounding vegetation. Hero designs for  
David IV and Tamar use the gold and red accents prominently, with David IV in military

3.5 Era 4: The Mongol Catastrophe
The Mongol Catastrophe palette is the darkest and most somber in the game, reflecting the  
destruction and despair of the Mongol conquest period. The base brown (#3D2B1F) is nearly  
black, representing burned and abandoned cities. The accent orange (#CC5500) is harsh and  
industrial, evoking fire and destruction rather than the warm gold of earlier eras. The  
vegetation color (#556B2F) is desaturated, suggesting scorched earth and dying crops.
Visual storytelling in this era emphasizes contrast between the ruined Georgian architecture  
and the imposing, utilitarian forms of Mongol military camps. Georgian towers appear  
damaged, with missing sections and smoke effects. Enemy designs for Mongol forces use a  
distinctive palette subset: dark leather armor in the Base range, fur-trimmed helmets in the  
Shadow range, and the accent orange for Mongol banners and fire effects. Maps feature  
dramatic, smoke-filled skies and barren landscapes interrupted by the occasional defiant  
mountain fortress.
3.6 Era 5: Between Empires
The Between Empires palette reflects the cosmopolitan character of Georgian culture during  
the Ottoman-Persian rivalry period. The blue base (#1A3C5E) represents the expanding  
influence of both empires on Georgian territory. The accent red (#E74C3C) is vivid and  
dramatic, symbolizing both the blood of constant warfare and the rich Persian-influenced  
textile  traditions  that  flourished  despite  political  instability.  The  warm  cream  sky  
(#F5DEB3) evokes the sun-drenched landscapes of eastern Georgia's wine country.
This era's visual language is defined by cultural fusion. Tower skins feature a blend of  
Georgian, Persian, and Ottoman architectural elements: Georgian stone foundations with  
Persian  tile  decorations  and  Ottoman-style  crenellations.  The  Gunpowder  tower  type,  
introduced in this era, uses the metallic Stone/Earth palette (#CD853F) for bronze cannon  
barrels and the accent red for muzzle flashes. Enemy designs alternate between Ottoman  
(blue-white palette subset) and Persian (blue-red palette subset) forces, each visually distinct.
3.7 Era 6: Russian Empire
The Russian Empire palette is cool and institutional, reflecting the period of annexation and  
the imposed European architectural influence on Georgian cities. The purple-gray base  
(#2C2C3E) represents the bureaucratic and melancholic character of the era, while the accent  
purple (#9B59B6) subtly references the Russian imperial aesthetic that was overlaid on

Tower skins in this era mark the transition from traditional Georgian military architecture to

3.8 Era 7: First Democratic Republic
The First Republic palette is surprisingly optimistic, using fresh greens and blues to reflect  
the hope and democratic aspirations of Georgia's brief independence period (1918-1921).  
The base green (#1B4332) is lighter and more vibrant than earlier military eras, suggesting  
renewal and national rebirth. The accent red (#E63946) represents both the revolutionary  
spirit and the sacrifice of the independence wars. The sky blue (#A8DADC) is clear and  
bright, conveying the openness and possibility of a new democratic state.
Visual design for this era bridges the gap between the Russian imperial aesthetic and the  
modern  world.  Tower  skins  feature  early  20th-century  military  architecture:  concrete  
bunkers, sandbag emplacements, and improvised fortifications. The transition from 19th-
century to 20th-century military technology is visually represented through the mixture of  
older stone structures with modern concrete additions. Enemy designs represent the multiple  
threats the republic faced simultaneously, each with distinct visual identities within the  
palette.
3.9 Era 8: The Soviet Century
The Soviet Century palette is stark and utilitarian, dominated by grays and the iconic Soviet  
red. The base gray (#2D2D2D) represents the concrete brutalism of Soviet architecture,  
while  the  accent  red  (#D32F2F)  is  the  unmistakable  symbol  of  Soviet  power.  The  
desaturated blue-gray sky (#90A4AE) conveys the oppressive atmosphere of the period, and  
the muted green vegetation (#558B2F) suggests controlled, utilitarian landscaping rather  
than natural beauty.
Tower skins in this era are the most standardized and industrial in the game. Reinforced  
concrete  bunkers,  anti-aircraft  positions,  and  fortified  checkpoints  replace  the  ornate  
structures of earlier eras. The Bunker tower type uses the concrete gray palette (#757575)  
with red Soviet stars as the only decorative element. Map backgrounds show Soviet-era

3.10 Era 9: Modern Georgia
The Modern Georgia palette is dark and sophisticated, using deep navy blues and strategic  
pops of red to convey a contemporary, forward-looking aesthetic. The base navy (#0D1B2A)  
represents  the  night-time  urban  environment  of  modern  Tbilisi,  while  the  accent  red  
(#E63946) connects to the Georgian flag and the ongoing national identity. The steel blue  
(#415A77) represents modern military technology and infrastructure.
Tower skins in this era feature cutting-edge military technology: precision-guided weapon  
systems,  drone  surveillance  platforms,  and  fortified  military  outposts  with  modern  
communications equipment. The Tech tower type uses a sleek, minimalist design language  
with the navy and steel blue palette. Map backgrounds show modern Tbilisi's mix of  
contemporary architecture and historic landmarks, the strategic Georgian Military Highway,  
and the dramatic mountain landscapes that define Georgia's geopolitical significance. The  
visual language conveys both the technological modernity of the present and the enduring  
weight of Georgian history.

identify tower types instantly during combat. The following guidelines define the silhouette

4.2 Silhouette Rules
1. No Overlap: No two tower types should share the same basic silhouette shape. If two  
towers  appear  identical  in  silhouette,  the  design  must  be  revised  until  they  are  
distinguishable.
2. Scale Consistency: All towers are designed on the same grid scale. A 10-unit tall Archer  
Tower and a 3-unit tall Wall/Barrier must appear correctly proportioned when placed next to  
each other on the same map.
3. Era Skin Layering: Era-specific skins are applied as a visual layer over the base silhouette.  
The  silhouette  shape  never  changes  across  eras;  only  the  surface  details,  colors,  and  
decorative elements vary. An Archer Tower in Era 0 (wooden watchtower) and an Archer  
Tower in Era 9 (modern guard post) must have the same pointed-tower silhouette.
4. Readability at Small Size: All tower designs must be tested at 64x64 pixel resolution. If a  
tower cannot be identified at this size, the design is too complex and must be simplified. Key  
identifying features (listed in the table above) must remain visible at minimum resolution.
5. Upgrade Visual Feedback:  Tower upgrades (Level 1 to Level 3) must be visually  
distinguishable. Level 2 adds minor decorative elements to the base silhouette. Level 3 adds  
significant visual changes: additional height, new structural elements, or glowing effects that  
make the upgrade status immediately obvious.

105. Character Proportion Reference
All humanoid characters (heroes, enemies, NPCs) follow a standardized 10-unit proportion  
system. This system ensures visual consistency across all AI-generated character art and  
provides clear guidelines for prompt engineering. The 10-unit system divides the character's  
total height into 10 equal units, with specific body parts occupying defined proportions.

5.2 Proportion Variations by Character Type
Heroes: 11 units total. Head is 2.5 units for greater facial detail visibility. Shoulders are 1.2  
units wider than standard. Torso is elongated by 0.5 units to convey authority. These subtle  
modifications make heroes visually distinct from standard units without breaking the overall  
proportion system.
Boss Enemies: 12-14 units total. Entire body scaled up uniformly by 20-40%. This uniform  
scaling ensures that boss silhouettes are immediately recognizable as larger versions of their  
unit type. Boss proportions follow the same 10-unit ratios applied to the larger frame.
Cavalry Units: 12 units total (10 for rider + 2 for horse head). Horse body extends 3 units  
behind the rider's center point. Cavalry proportions only apply to the rider; the horse follows  
separate animal proportion guidelines.

6. Hero Portrait Guidelines
Hero portraits are the most detailed character art in the game, appearing in the hero selection  
screen, level loading screens, and the collection gallery. Each portrait is a half-body or three-
quarter  view  composition  that  showcases  the  hero's  era-specific  clothing,  distinctive  
features, and personality. Portraits must work at both large sizes (gallery view) and small  
sizes (selection thumbnails, 80x80 pixels).
6.1 Composition Rules
Framing: Half-body composition, cropped at the waist. The hero's face occupies the upper  
third of the portrait, centered horizontally. The hero's body is angled 15-30 degrees from the  
camera (three-quarter view), never a flat profile or direct front-facing pose.
Background: A blurred, desaturated version of the hero's era palette as a gradient. The hero's  
Accent color appears as a subtle radial glow behind the head, creating visual separation from  
the background without adding a visible halo effect.
Expression:  Each hero has a defined expression that reflects their personality. Military  
heroes (David IV, Erekle II) have determined, focused expressions. Cultural heroes (Ilia  
Chavchavadze,  Rustaveli)  have  thoughtful,  contemplative  expressions.  Support  heroes  
(Medea, Saint Nino) have calm, serene expressions. No hero has an aggressive or angry  
expression; even in a war game, the tone is dignified rather than hostile.
Era Markers: Each portrait includes 2-3 small era-identifying elements in the background or  
clothing details: a cross for Christian-era heroes, a crown for royalty, a manuscript for  
literary figures, military insignia for commanders. These markers must be subtle enough to

7.2 Enemy Color Rules
Enemies use a deliberately restricted palette subset to create visual contrast with player  
towers. While towers use the full 8-color era palette, enemies are rendered primarily in the

readability while communicating faction identity.

137.3 Cultural Sensitivity Standards
Enemy designs must never use exaggerated ethnic features, caricatured facial structures, or  
stereotypical clothing that could be interpreted as mocking a real cultural group. Enemies are  
military units, depicted with the same dignity and accuracy as player units. Historical  
accuracy means showing real military equipment and tactics of the period, not reinforcing  
ethnic stereotypes. This principle is especially important for eras involving ongoing political  
sensitivities (Era 8 Soviet period, Era 9 modern conflicts) where enemy designs must be  
factually accurate and neutrally presented.
8. Map Design Guidelines
Maps are the visual backdrop for gameplay, providing the context for tower placement,  
enemy pathing, and strategic decision-making. Each of the 200 levels has a unique map, but  
maps within each era share visual characteristics defined by the era palette and the map  
design rules below. Maps must be visually interesting without distracting from the gameplay  
elements (towers, enemies, projectiles) that are overlaid on them.
8.1 Map Composition
Layered Depth: Maps use three depth layers: foreground (interactive zone where towers are  
placed),  midground  (enemy  path  with  terrain  features),  and  background  (sky,  distant  
mountains, horizon). The foreground occupies the bottom 40% of the screen, the midground  
the middle 35%, and the background the top 25%. This proportion ensures that the interactive  
zone  is  the  largest  and  most  detailed  area  while  maintaining  a  sense  of  depth  and  
environment.
Path Visibility: Enemy paths must be clearly visible against the terrain. Paths are rendered as  
lighter or darker strips (depending on terrain brightness) that contrast with the surrounding  
vegetation. Path width is consistent within a map but may vary between maps (narrow  
mountain passes vs. wide plains). Paths never overlap or cross at the same elevation; multi-
path maps use elevation differences (bridges, tunnels) for crossings.
Placement Zones:  Tower placement zones are indicated by subtle grid markers on the  
foreground  terrain.  These  markers  are  visible  enough  to  guide  placement  but  not  so  
prominent that they dominate the visual composition. Grid markers use the era's Shadow  
color at 30% opacity, creating a faint guide pattern that blends with the terrain texture.

148.2 Elevation Rendering
Maps use visual elevation cues to communicate the height mechanic. Elevated positions are  
rendered with a visible base/foundation that shows the height difference from the ground  
level. The foundation uses the era's Stone/Earth color, and the elevated platform uses the  
era's Base color, creating a clear visual distinction between ground-level and elevated terrain.  
Towers placed on elevated positions appear visually higher on the screen, with a visible  
shadow beneath them indicating their height advantage.
Elevation levels follow a simple two-tier system: ground level and elevated. Elevated  
positions are always on the midground layer, creating a natural visual separation from  
ground-level  placement  zones.  Maps  typically  have  2-4  elevated  positions,  providing  
meaningful but not overwhelming strategic options. The visual height difference between  
ground and elevated positions is approximately 20-30% of the map's total height, making the  
elevation clearly visible without disrupting the isometric perspective.

9. UI Design Language
The UI design language defines the visual treatment of all in-game interface elements:  
buttons, panels, health bars, resource counters, hero ability cooldowns, and notification  
systems. The UI language is era-independent (always using the Global UI Palette from  
Section 2) but adapts its border style and background opacity to complement the current era's  
visual character.
9.1 UI Components
Buttons:  Rounded rectangles with 8px border radius. Primary buttons use Accent fill  
(#E67E22) with white text. Secondary buttons use Panel Background fill with Primary text.  
All buttons have a 2px border in Border color. Hover state brightens fill by 15%. Press state  
darkens fill by 10% and shifts content 1px down. Disabled state uses Disabled fill with  
Disabled text.
Panels:  Rounded rectangles with 12px border radius. Panel Background fill with Border  
color stroke. Panels have a subtle inner shadow (2px, 10% opacity) to create depth. Panels  
containing era-specific content (tower info, hero details) include a thin accent strip (3px) at  
the top edge using the current era's Accent color.
Health Bars: Horizontal bars with 4px height, rounded ends. Full health uses Success green  
(#27AE60). Mid health (50-80%) uses Info blue (#3498DB). Low health (under 50%) uses  
Danger red (#E74C3C). Background bar uses Disabled color. Health bars include a 1px dark  
outline for readability against any background.
Resource Counters: Icon + number combination, right-aligned in the HUD. Icon is 24x24px  
in the appropriate color (gold for SAKART, blue for in-game resources, red for lives).  
Number uses Primary Text color with a subtle text shadow for readability. Counters update  
with a smooth counting animation (not instant jumps).

169.2 HUD Layout
The heads-up display (HUD) occupies the top and bottom edges of the screen, leaving the  
central area clear for gameplay. The top HUD bar contains: level timer (left), wave counter  
(center), and pause/settings buttons (right). The bottom HUD bar contains: resource counters  
(left), hero ability buttons with cooldown indicators (center), and scroll inventory access  
(right). The HUD is semi-transparent (80% opacity Panel Background) to not obscure the  
game view.
The HUD design follows the invisible blockchain principle. SAKART-related UI elements  
(token balance, marketplace access) are accessible from a secondary menu, not displayed  
prominently  in  the  gameplay  HUD.  The  gameplay  HUD  shows  only  game-relevant  
information: in-game resources, lives, wave progress, and hero abilities. This separation  
ensures that the gameplay experience feels like a traditional tower defense game regardless  
of blockchain engagement.
9.3 Notification System
Notifications use a consistent toast system: brief messages that appear at the top-center of the  
screen, slide down with a smooth animation, remain visible for 3 seconds, then slide up and  
fade out. Success notifications use Success green. Error notifications use Danger red.  
Information notifications use Info blue. Warning notifications use Accent orange. All  
notifications have a dark semi-transparent background for readability and a 2px left-border  
accent stripe in the appropriate color.

11. Scroll and Mastery Visual Design
Scrolls and mastery scrolls are visual items that appear in the player's inventory, the shop,  
and the marketplace. Their design must be distinctive, readable at small sizes, and clearly  
communicate their function and rarity. The visual design language for scrolls draws on  
Georgian manuscript illumination traditions, particularly the distinctive calligraphic style of  
medieval Georgian gospel books.
11.1 Standard Scroll Design
Standard scrolls are rendered as rectangular cards with a parchment texture background. The  
card dimensions are 3:4 aspect ratio (portrait orientation). The top 30% of the card contains  
the scroll type icon (a simple, symbolic representation of the scroll's effect: a flame for  
damage, a coin for economy, a snowflake for crowd control, a heart for healing, a shield for  
protection). The middle 40% contains the scroll's name in a clean sans-serif font. The bottom  
30% contains a brief one-line description in secondary text color.
Scroll rarity is indicated by a colored border: Common (gray), Uncommon (green), Rare  
(blue), Epic (purple). The rarity border is 3px wide with a subtle glow effect (era accent color  
at 20% opacity). The parchment background color is consistent across all rarities (#F5E6C8)  
to maintain the manuscript aesthetic. Stack count (when applicable) is shown as a small  
badge in the top-right corner of the card.

2011.2 Mastery Scroll Design
Mastery scrolls use a more elaborate card design than standard scrolls, reflecting their status  
as blockchain assets (ERC-721 NFTs). The card dimensions are the same 3:4 ratio but with  
additional visual elements. The background uses a darker parchment (#D4C4A0) with a  
subtle geometric pattern overlay (Georgian cross motif at 5% opacity) to communicate the  
blockchain connection without using crypto terminology.
Mastery scroll cards include: the scroll type icon (larger and more detailed than standard  
scrolls), the tower or hero it affects (shown as a small silhouette), the bonus percentage (3%,  
6%, or 9% depending on stack count), and a small blockchain verification indicator (a  
stylized shield icon) in the top-right corner. The card border uses the accent gold color  
(#D4AF37) to visually distinguish mastery scrolls from standard scrolls. When a mastery  
scroll is activated (equipped for a level), the card gains a subtle pulsing glow effect.

versions are archived with their era and type codes intact.
The  Art  Style  Guide  itself  follows  semantic  versioning:  major  versions  (v1.0,  v2.0)  
correspond to Bible versions and involve significant structural changes (era additions, palette

2213. Consistency Checklist
Every AI-generated asset must pass the following checklist before being accepted into the  
game. The checklist is applied during the review process and documented in the asset log.  
Any asset that fails a checklist item must be regenerated or manually corrected before  
inclusion.
13.1 Color Compliance
All visible colors in the asset match the era palette exactly (Section 3). No unauthorized color  
substitutions. Colors are verified by sampling pixel values from the generated image and  
comparing them to the hex values in the palette. Tolerance: plus or minus 10 units per RGB  
channel  to  account  for  AI  rendering  variation.  Colors  outside  this  tolerance  require  
regeneration.
13.2 Silhouette Compliance
Tower silhouettes match the reference shapes defined in Section 4. The silhouette is verified  
by converting the asset to a solid black silhouette and comparing it against the reference  
silhouette at 64x64 pixel resolution. The silhouette match must be at least 90% similar  
(measured by pixel overlap). Silhouettes that differ significantly indicate an AI generation  
error.
13.3 Proportion Compliance
Humanoid characters follow the 10-unit proportion system defined in Section 5. Proportions  
are verified by measuring key body landmarks (head top, chin, shoulders, waist, feet) and  
confirming that each segment falls within the defined unit range. Tolerance: plus or minus  
0.3 units per segment.
13.4 Style Compliance
The asset matches the 2D isometric cel-shaded style defined in Section 1. Key checks  
include: consistent shadow direction (lower-right, 45 degrees), correct outline weight (2px  
for objects, 3px for characters), flat color fills (no unintended gradients), and correct lighting  
(highlights on upper-left surfaces). Any deviation from the style rules requires regeneration.
13.5 Era Compliance
Architectural details, clothing, military equipment, and environmental elements match the  
era keyword library (Section 10.2). Anachronistic elements (a gun in Era 0, a smartphone in

--- Page 27 ---

23Era 3) are immediate failures. Era compliance is verified by comparing the asset against the  
era keyword list and confirming that all visible elements are period-appropriate.
13.6 Technical Quality
The asset has no visible artifacts: no JPEG compression artifacts, no blurry edges, no  
unintended transparency, no text or watermark overlays, and no AI generation artifacts (extra  
fingers, merged objects, distorted perspective). Resolution meets the minimum standard:  
512x512 pixels for towers, 1024x1024 for hero portraits, 2048x2048 for map backgrounds.
13.7 Cultural Sensitivity Review
The asset does not contain caricatured ethnic features, stereotypical depictions of any  
cultural group, or historically inaccurate representations that could cause offense. Enemy  
designs  depict  military  units  with  dignity  and  accuracy.  Religious  symbols  are  used  
respectfully and in historically appropriate contexts. Any asset that raises cultural sensitivity  
concerns is escalated for review before inclusion.
