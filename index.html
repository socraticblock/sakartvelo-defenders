Historical Accuracy Policy

We label what kind of truth each story uses.

This is not a textbook, but Georgian history deserves respect. When a detail is documented, mythic, debated, or adapted for gameplay, the game should say so clearly.

H

History

Documented events, people, places, and long-standing historical traditions presented with careful wording.

A

Archaeology

Material culture, sites, artifacts, landscapes, and visual design based on archaeological evidence.

M

Myth

Medea, Aeetes, the Golden Fleece, devi, dragons, and folklore are treated as mythic memory, not battlefield records.

G

Game Adaptation

Invented battles, towers, abilities, and boss mechanics are clearly framed as playable dramatization.

Current Direction

Ancient Colchis without fake certainty.

Era 0: Ancient Colchis and Bronze-Age Roots

The active game uses a broad playable arc from c. 1500 BC to 83 BC. Early chapters draw on Bronze-Age and proto-Colchian roots; later chapters focus on the Kingdom of Colchis known to Greek sources and archaeology.

Public wording: “Ancient Colchis and its Bronze-Age roots,” not “every level is a documented battle.”

Medea as mythic guide

Medea is presented as a mythic Colchian princess and symbolic hero-guide. Her abilities are game mechanics inspired by mythic associations with herbs, ritual knowledge, and transformation.

Public wording: “mythic guide,” not “verified commander.”
