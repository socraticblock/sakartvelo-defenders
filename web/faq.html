Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Sakartvelo Defenders, from gameplay to blockchain mechanics.

Gameplay

What is Sakartvelo Defenders?

Sakartvelo Defenders is a tower defense game spanning 10 historical eras and 200 levels of Georgian history. Players build mazes, place towers, and command heroes from Georgian legend to defend against waves of enemies. It's a cultural celebration through historical gameplay.

How do I unlock new eras?

Complete 15 of 20 levels in the current era to unlock the next. Progression is purely skill-based — no fees or payments required. Each era brings new tower types, enemies, heroes, maps, and gameplay mechanics.

How do heroes work?

Heroes are legendary figures from Georgian history that you can directly control. Each hero has 2-3 active abilities with cooldowns. For example, Medea can create toxic clouds, Queen Tamar boosts global tower damage, and David the Builder instantly upgrades towers. Heroes are earned through gameplay, not purchased.

What platforms will the game be available on?

The game is built as a Progressive Web App (PWA) using Godot 4.x, meaning it runs in any modern browser. It's installable on mobile devices (iOS and Android) without going through app stores. This keeps costs at $100/month and ensures maximum accessibility.

Is the game historically accurate?

Yes, we've worked closely with historians to ensure accuracy. Tower types, enemy formations, hero abilities, and map designs are all drawn from real Georgian history. The game features 125 cultural facts, 200 historical pop-ups, and authentic representations of each era. This isn't "culture as skin" — history drives the gameplay.

Is there a No-Nerf Rule?

Yes. When something is too strong, traditional games nerf it. We don't. If the community discovers a strategy that breaks the game, we ask: "Why does this work? Is it fun for everyone?" If yes, we balance around it — adding counter-strategies rather than removing player agency. Players who master a system should keep their mastery.

Blockchain & SAKART Token

Do I need to understand crypto to play?

No. The blockchain is invisible infrastructure. Players log in with email, and a wallet is created automatically using Immutable's Passport. No MetaMask tutorials, no seed phrases, no gas fees. You click "Play" and earn tokens naturally through skilled gameplay.

What are SAKART tokens used for?

SAKART tokens are earned by three-starring levels on your first playthrough. They can be used to purchase cosmetic skins (ERC-1155), trade on the marketplace, or stake for rewards. Importantly, SAKART does not affect gameplay — every gameplay-affecting item is earned through skill, not purchased.

Is the game pay-to-win?

Absolutely not. Every gameplay-affecting item (towers, upgrades, heroes, scrolls) is earned through skill. A player who spends $0 has the exact same experience as someone who spends $1,000. SAKART tokens are earned by three-starring levels, not by swiping a credit card. This is a core design principle.

What blockchain is the game on?

The game runs on Immutable zkEVM, a Layer 2 Ethereum scaling solution. This means zero gas fees for players, fast transactions, and full compatibility with Ethereum. Immutable's Passport wallet abstraction makes onboarding seamless — email login, automatic wallet creation.

Technical & Business

How is this sustainable on $100/month?

Game costs: Godot 4.x is free, PWA means no server costs ($0), Vercel hosting is free. Revenue comes from a 1.8% marketplace fee on SAKART and skin trades. This scales naturally with player activity — more players = more marketplace trades = more revenue. It's a self-sustaining economy designed for solo-dev feasibility.

When will the game launch?

We're currently in early development. Join the waitlist to be notified when the full campaign launches with all 10 eras and 200 levels. We'll share updates on our Discord and Twitter as development progresses.

Can I contribute to the project?

Yes! This is an open-source project. Check out our GitHub repository to contribute code, report bugs, or suggest features. We also welcome historians and cultural experts to help ensure accuracy. Join our Discord to connect with the community.

Will there be multiplayer?

Yes, we're planning asynchronous PvP where players compete with identical loadouts. No pay-to-win, no grinding — pure skill-based competition. We're also exploring cooperative modes and leaderboards. Join the waitlist for updates on these features.

How does the PvP system work?

Asynchronous attack-then-defend format. Both players get identical loadouts (same towers, heroes, scrolls). Rock-Paper-Scissors decides who attacks first. During the attack phase, you send waves against the opponent's preset defense. During the defend phase, your defense fights their waves. Winner is determined by total score across both phases. Weekly hero and scroll selection keeps the meta fresh.

What are mastery scrolls?

Mastery scrolls are permanent ERC-721 NFT bonuses that stack up to 3 times per type (+3%, +6%, +9%). Unlike standard consumable scrolls, mastery scrolls are tradeable on the marketplace and never consumed. Examples: "Archer Tower Mastery" (+9% archer damage), "Cooldown Reduction" (+9% ability cooldown speed). They're earned through gameplay achievements and provide persistent progression outside the main campaign.

Chronicle & Education

History & Mythology

What is the Chronicle?

The Chronicle is the game's educational deep-dive -- a companion reference covering all 10 eras of Georgian history. It includes verified historical events, key battles, cultural achievements, and the mythology that inspires the game's enemies and heroes. Think of it as the encyclopedia that makes the game meaningful.

Is the historical content accurate?

Yes. The game is built on verified sources including Kartlis Tskhovreba (Life of Kartli) medieval chronicles, English-language academic scholarship, UNESCO records, and scholarly databases. When the historical record is ambiguous, the game acknowledges uncertainty rather than presenting speculation as fact. The v4 bible corrected 5 historical errors and clarified 8 disputed claims from earlier versions.

What mythology is in the game?

Georgian mythology enriches early eras: Devi are many-headed ogres with regeneration mechanics (not generic monsters). Amirani is the Georgian Prometheus, chained to Mount Kazbek -- a seasonal hero with a "Stolen Fire" ability. Kaji are evil spirits and demonic metalworkers (not benign blacksmiths). The Golden Fleece has a historical basis in Svaneti's gold-panning with sheep fleeces. The Borjgali solar symbol appears throughout the game's visual design.

How is the Stalin era handled?

Factually, without glorification. Stalin is mentioned in historical context as part of Era 8 (The Soviet Century). The game covers the 1956 Tbilisi Massacre, WWII's impact on Georgia (~190,000 killed in action), and the independence movement objectively. The focus remains on the Georgian people's experience, not on any individual's legacy.

Development & Sustainability

Behind the Game

Can one developer really build this?

Yes -- that's the design. Godot 4.x is free and purpose-built for 2D games. PWA architecture means zero server costs and no app store friction. An AI-assisted art pipeline handles asset creation. The game is designed to be completed solo: 10 eras released incrementally, each building on proven systems. Phase 1 (Eras 0-1) ships first, then subsequent eras follow based on community engagement.

What happens if the blockchain goes down?

The game continues. Chain outages trigger a local queuing system that stores transactions until the network recovers. Players never lose progress. The blockchain is infrastructure for ownership and trading -- the core gameplay runs locally in the browser regardless of network status.

How do you prevent multi-account farming?

Three layers: Immutable Passport identity verification ties each account to a real identity. The 15/20 level progression requirement creates a significant time barrier for each account. Marketplace monitoring detects coordinated trading patterns between suspected alt accounts. Multiple indicators are required before action -- conservative enforcement prevents false positives.
