## Era 0: Ancient Colchis (~1500 BC - ~300 BC)

The land of gold, myth, and first kingdoms. Colchis sits at the crossroads of Greek, Anatolian, and Caucasian civilizations. This is where the story of Sakartvelo begins — with legendary kings, the myth of the Golden Fleece, and goldsmiths whose work rivaled Egypt's finest.

**Key Figures:** King Aeetes, Medea, Kuji
**Key Events:** The Argonautic Expedition, Greek Colonization, Rise of Vani
**Defining Achievement:** Colchian gold metallurgy

## Era 1: Kingdom of Iberia (~300 BC - ~630 AD)

The crucible of Georgian identity. Iberia unifies the eastern Georgian tribes, adopts Christianity, and invents one of the world's few independent alphabets. Caught between Rome and Persia, Iberia develops the resilience that defines Georgia for millennia.

**Key Figures:** King Pharnavaz I, King Mirian III, Saint Nino, King Vakhtang Gorgasali, Peter the Iberian
**Key Events:** Christianization (~327 AD), Invention of Georgian Alphabet, Byzantine-Persian Wars
**Defining Achievement:** The Georgian alphabet and early literature

## Era 2: Age of Invasions (~630 - ~1089 AD)

Arab conquest, Seljuk devastation, and the slow fight back. Georgia is occupied, divided, and battered — but never broken. Monasteries in the mountains preserve culture, and the Bagrationi dynasty begins the long work of reunification.

**Key Figures:** Ashot I, David III Kuropalates, King Bagrat III, Giorgi II
**Key Events:** Arab Conquest and Tbilisi Emirate, Battle of Sasireti, Seljuk Invasions
**Defining Achievement:** Tao-Klarjeti monastic renaissance

## Era 3: Georgian Golden Age (~1089 - ~1225 AD)

The peak. David the Builder drives out the Seljuks. Queen Tamar commands an empire from the Black Sea to the Caspian. Shota Rustaveli writes Georgia's national epic. This is what Georgia can achieve at its best.

**Key Figures:** David IV "the Builder," Demetre I, Queen Tamar, Shota Rustaveli
**Key Events:** Battle of Didgori (1121), Capture of Tbilisi (1122), Battles of Shamkor (1195) and Basian (1203)
**Defining Achievement:** "The Knight in the Panther's Skin" and Gelati Academy

## Era 4: Mongol Catastrophe (~1225 - ~1500 AD)

Mongol hordes destroy everything. Timur sacks Tbilisi eight times. The population collapses. But in the mountains of Svaneti, monks preserve manuscripts, and tower-fortresses keep the flame alive. This is Georgia's darkest hour — and its greatest test of endurance.

**Key Figures:** Queen Rusudan, David VI Narin, Demetre II "the Self-Sacrificer," George V "the Brilliant," Timur
**Key Events:** Mongol Invasion (1236-1243), Kingdom Division, Timur's Invasions (1386-1403), Battle of Torgha (1444)
**Defining Achievement:** Survival of manuscripts and development of tower architecture

## Era 5: Between Empires (~1500 - ~1801 AD)

Ottomans to the west, Persians to the east, Russians to the north. Georgia's kingdoms navigate this impossible landscape through diplomacy, warfare, and cultural brilliance. King Erekle II's gamble on Russia sets the stage for the next era.

**Key Figures:** David X of Kartli, King Rostom, King Erekle II, Saint Ketevan
**Key Events:** Ottoman Conquest, Treaty of Georgievsk (1783), Persian Sack of Tbilisi (1795)
**Defining Achievement:** Persian-influenced art and architecture; first Georgian printing press

## Era 6: Russian Empire (~1801 - ~1918 AD)

Georgia joins the Russian Empire — modernization at the cost of freedom. Roads, railways, and schools are built, but the Georgian Church loses its independence and the royal family is exiled. Ilia Chavchavadze leads the national revival.

**Key Figures:** General Ermolov, Ilia Chavchavadze, Niko Nikoladze, Nino Chavchavadze, Princess Ekaterine Dadiani
**Key Events:** Annexation (1801), Abolition of Church Autocephaly (1811), 1832 Conspiracy, 1905 Revolution
**Defining Achievement:** The national revival and modern Georgian literature

## Era 7: First Democratic Republic (1918-1921)

Three years of democracy, then crushed by the Red Army. Georgia becomes one of the first European countries to grant women the vote. The republic builds a constitution, reforms agriculture, and joins the League of Nations — but cannot survive the Bolshevik onslaught.

**Key Figures:** Noe Zhordania, Noe Ramishvili, Kato Meskhi, General Giorgi Kvinitadze
**Key Events:** Declaration of Independence (1918), Treaty of Moscow (1920), Soviet-Georgian War (1921), League of Nations Admission (1921)
**Defining Achievement:** Democratic constitution with universal suffrage

## Era 8: Soviet Century (1921-1991)

Seventy years of transformation and trauma. Industrialization, mass education, world-class cinema — alongside purges, religious persecution, and the suppression of freedom. A Georgian (Stalin) rules the Soviet Union, but his policies devastate his homeland. The April 9 tragedy ignites the final drive for independence.

**Key Figures:** Joseph Stalin, Zviad Gamsakhurdia, Merab Kostava, Eduard Shevardnadze
**Key Events:** Red Army Invasion (1921), Great Purge (1937-1938), World War II, April 9 Tragedy (1989)
**Defining Achievement:** Georgian cinema and polyphonic music

📄 [Read Full Entry →](encyclopedia_era_8.md)

## Era 9: Modern Georgia (~1991-Present)

Independence restored — but at enormous cost. Civil war, secessionist conflicts, and economic collapse give way to the Rose Revolution, EU candidacy, and a growing reputation as a cultural and tourist destination. Georgia's story continues.

**Key Figures:** Zviad Gamsakhurdia, Eduard Shevardnadze, Mikheil Saakashvili, Bidzina Ivanishvili, Salome Zourabichvili
**Key Events:** Rose Revolution (2003), August 2008 War, EU Candidate Status (2023)
**Defining Achievement:** Post-Soviet cultural revival and the Georgian wine renaissance
